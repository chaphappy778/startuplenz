## Body (~330 words)

This is the first product I've ever shipped to real users, so being honest about it up front: I'm not a serial founder with five SaaS launches behind me. I had a problem at my kitchen table and built a tool for it.

My wife was getting into slimes and wanted to know if her own slime brand would actually pencil out, so before investing she built a spreadsheet to model it: materials, packaging, TikTok Shop fees, summer heat-pack costs. The works.

I looked at what she'd built and thought "this same pattern would work for any DIY business someone is considering." Could we model a food truck the same way? A cleaning service? A subscription box? Turns out yes, once you commit to one model per vertical instead of pretending they're all the same kind of business.

That's how **[StartupLenz](https://www.startuplenz.com)** ended up at ten verticals (slime, food truck, candle, subscription box, print-on-demand, digital products, cleaning service, handmade, reseller, house flipping) with more on the way.

The thing that makes it different: the defaults aren't hardcoded. They live in a database and update as marketplace fees, materials prices, and channel economics shift. When Etsy bumps a fee or TikTok Shop's average sell-through moves, the calculators reflect it. Most cost calculators get less accurate every month after they ship. I built the living pathways so this one doesn't.

A few other things I tried to do right:
- Channel-aware revenue (model the Etsy / TikTok / own-site split, not an averaged blob)
- A written-take insight, not just charts (the model engine reads your numbers and tells you the most relevant thing to fix first)
- Free, no email gate, no "upgrade to see your full report" nonsense

I'd love feedback on:
1. The slime model specifically. If you've operated in that space, are the defaults reasonable?
2. What vertical you wish was modeled next. Service businesses (cleaning, lawncare, beauty, mobile auto-detail) are coming in the next batch.

Try it: **[www.startuplenz.com](https://www.startuplenz.com)**. Pick a vertical, slide some sliders, tell me what's wrong.
